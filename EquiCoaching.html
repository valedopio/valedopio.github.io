What is EquiCoaching?


EquiCoaching (or equine-assisted coaching) uses the non-verbal language of horses as mirrors for your internal state. Without force or instruction, the horse responds to your energy, presence, and intentions. Through guided interaction, you gain insight into your behavior patterns, communication style, emotional blockages, and leadership qualities.

“I came feeling stressed and disconnected. Within minutes, the horse reflected exactly how I was showing up — and invited me to soften. I left lighter, more grounded, and somehow more myself.”


How It Works

Intake & Intention Setting


We begin by talking: exploring what you wish to learn, shift or understand. This gives direction and focus for the session.

Groundwork


With the Horse
Under gentle guidance, you interact with the horse — perhaps leading, engaging, or simply relating in silence. The horse becomes a non-judgmental feedback partner.

Reflective Processing

After the interaction, we reflect together. What did you notice? What did the horse invite you to shift? What internal theme was triggered?


Application & Integration


We close by drawing connections to your everyday life. You leave with insights and possible practices to integrate what surfaced.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need horse experience to join?
Not at all. Our sessions are done safely on the ground — no riding is involved. What matters most is curiosity and openness to learn from the horses.

What should I wear or bring?
Comfortable clothes and closed shoes are ideal. Bring a hat, sunscreen, and a water bottle if it’s sunny. Everything else is provided here at Vale do Pio.

Can I come with a friend or partner?
Yes, we offer shared or duo sessions on request. These can be a beautiful way to explore communication and connection together.

Is it therapy?
EquiCoaching is a personal development experience rather than psychotherapy. It can, however, be deeply therapeutic in its effects — helping you gain awareness, calm, and inner clarity.

What if I’m afraid of horses?
That’s absolutely fine. Many participants start with some fear. We always move at your pace, in a safe and supportive way. Often, working gently through that fear brings the most powerful insights.
